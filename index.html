Knowledge Base

Frequently Asked Questions

The Thailand Elite Privilege Visa is a special visa introduced in 2003 by the Tourism Authority of Thailand, aimed at attracting affluent visitors, businessmen, investors, and expats to stay in Thailand long-term. It is classified as a Privilege Entry Visa, valid for 5–20 years with multiple entries without extensive paperwork or visits to the immigration office. Holders are permitted to stay in the country for up to 1 year on each visit.
The Thailand Privilege Membership and Thai Privilege Visa application process may take 4 weeks to 3 months, depending on the nationality of the applicant and any immigration or criminal record history. Our team works to expedite the process wherever possible.

Ideally, you need at least 5 years remaining on your passport. However, if you have less than 5 years, the Thai Embassy or Immigration Bureau will affix the Thai Privilege Visas based on the expiration date in your passport. The remaining validity will then be affixed to your new passport when renewed. You should also have at least 3 blank pages in your passport.
No, it is not required to personally appear during the Thai Privilege membership application. You will be represented by a General Sales and Services Agent (GSSA) during the membership application. However, your presence is required during the issuance of the visa, whether it is at the Thai Embassy abroad, at Suvarnabhumi airport, or at the Bangkok immigration office.
